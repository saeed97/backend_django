Step Size Strategy for The Numerical Integration of Systems of Non-Linear Differential Equations


Consider the Cauchy problem \begin{equation*} X'(t)=AX(t)+\varphi(t,X),\ X(t_{0})=X_{0} \end{equation*} on $D=\{(t,x): |t-t_{0}|\leq a, |x_{j}-x_{j0}|\leq b_{j} \}$. Local error in $ith$ step for the Cauchy problem is \begin{equation*} |LE_{i}|:=|Y_{i}-Z(t_{i})|=\dfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{N}h_{i}^{2}\{N^{2}\alpha^{2}\beta_{i-1}+N\alpha\gamma_{i-1}+\zeta_{i-1}\} \end{equation*} The step size in the $ith$ step has been obtained as follows \begin{equation*} h_{i}=N^{-0.25}\left(\frac{2\delta_{L}}{N^{2}\alpha^{2}\beta_{i-1}+N\alpha\gamma_{i-1}+\zeta_{i-1}}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} \end{equation*} such as local error is smaller than the desired error level $\delta_{L}$ in each step of the integration.

  • $\delta_{L}$; the desired error level

  • $N$; dimension of $A=(a_{ij})\in\mathbb{R}^{NxN}$ matrix, $\varphi \in C^{1}([t_{0}-a,t_{0}+a] \times \mathbb{R}^{N})$

  • $Y_{i}$; the numerical solution obtained in $ith$ step

  • $Z(t)$; the solution of $Z'(t)=AZ(t)+\varphi(t,Z(t)),\ Z(t_{i-1})=Y_{i-1},\ \tau_{i}\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})$

  • $\displaystyle\alpha=\max_{1\leq i,j \leq N}|a_{ij}|$, $\displaystyle\max_{1\leq i,j \leq N}\left(\sup_{\tau_{i}\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})}\left|z_{j}(\tau_{i})\right|\right)\leq\beta_{i-1}$

  • $\displaystyle\max_{1\leq j \leq N}\left(\sup_{\tau_{i}\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})}\left|\varphi_{j}(\tau_{i},z(\tau_{i}))\right|\right)\leq\gamma_{i-1}$, $\displaystyle\max_{1\leq j \leq N}\left(\sup_{\tau_{i}\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})}\left|\frac{d\varphi_{j}}{dt}(\tau_{i},z(\tau_{i}))\right|\right)\leq\zeta_{i-1}$
