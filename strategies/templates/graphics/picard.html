Step Size Strategy Based on Picard Theorem


Consider the Cauchy problem \begin{equation*} x'(t)=f(t,x(t)),\ x(t_{0})=x_{0} \end{equation*} on $D=\{(t,x): |t-t_{0}|\leq a, |x-x_{0}|\leq b \}$ where $f$ is a differentiable function. If the existence and uniqueness of the solution of the Cauchy problem are known, the step size in the $ith$ step has been obtained as follows

    \begin{equation*} h_{i}= \min\{ a,\frac{b}{M_{i}} \} \end{equation*}

by applying successively Picard Theorem.

  • $y_{i}$; the numerical solution obtained in $ith$ step

  • $D_{i}=\{(t,y): |t-t_{i}|\leq a, |y-y_{i}|\leq b\}$

  • $\displaystyle\max_{(\zeta,\xi)\in D_{i}}|f(\zeta,\xi)|\leq M_{i}$

Çelik Kızılkan 2004
