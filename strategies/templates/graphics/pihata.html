Step Size Strategy Based on Picard Theorem and Error Analysis


Consider the Cauchy problem \begin{equation} x'(t)=f(t,x(t)),\ x(t_{0})=x_{0} \end{equation} on $D=\{(t,x): |t-t_{0}|\leq a, |x-x_{0}|\leq b \}$ where $f$ is a differentiable function. If the existence of the solution of the Cauchy problem is unknown; the step size in the $ith$ step has been obtained as follows

    \begin{equation*} h_{i}= \min\{a, \frac{b_{0i-1}}{M_{i}}\} \end{equation*}

  • $y_{i}$; the numerical solution obtained in $ith$ step

  • $z(t)$; the solution of Cauchy problem $ z'(t)=f(t,z(t)),\ z(t_{i-1})=y_{i-1},\ t\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})$

  • $b_{i-1}$ is the upper bound of $|z-y_{i-1}|$

  • $b_{0i-1}=\min\{b_{0i-2}, b_{i-1}\}$

  • $D_{i-1}=\{(t,z): |t-t_{i-1}|\leq a, |z-y_{i-1}|\leq b_{0i-1} \}$

  • $\displaystyle\max_{(t,z)\in D_{i-1}}|f(t,z)|\leq M_{i}$
