Step Size Strategy Based on Error Analysis


Consider the Cauchy problem \begin{equation*} x'(t)=f(t,x(t)),\ x(t_{0})=x_{0} \end{equation*} on $D=\{(t,x): |t-t_{0}|\leq a, |x-x_{0}|\leq b \}$ where $f$ is a differentiable function. Local error in $ith$ step for the Cauchy problem is \begin{equation*} |LE_{i}|:=|y_{i}-z(t_{i})|=|\dfrac{1}{2!}z''(\tau)h_{i}^{2}|,\ \tau\in[t_{i-1},t_{i}) \end{equation*}

If the existence and uniqueness of the solution of the Cauchy problem are known, the step size in the $ith$ step has been obtained as follows

    \begin{equation*} h_{i}= \sqrt{ \dfrac{2\delta_{L}}{M_{t_{i}}}} \end{equation*}

such as local error is smaller than the desired error level $\delta_{L}$ in each step of the integration.

  • $\delta_{L}$; the desired error level

  • $y_{i}$; the numerical solution obtained in $ith$ step

  • $z(t)$; the solution of Cauchy problem $ z'(t)=f(t,z(t)),\ z(t_{i-1})=y_{i-1},\ t\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})$

  • $\displaystyle\max_{\tau\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})}|z''(\tau)|\leq M_{t_{i}}$

Çelik Kızılkan 2004 ,   Çelik Kızılkan and Aydın 2005
