Step Size Strategy for The Numerical Integration of Systems of Linear Differential Equations


Consider the Cauchy problem \begin{equation*} X'(t)=AX(t),\ X(t_{0})=X_{0} \end{equation*} on $D=\{(t,x): |t-t_{0}|\leq a, |x_{j}-x_{j0}|\leq b_{j} \}$. Local error in $ith$ step for the Cauchy problem is \begin{equation*} |LE_{i}|:=|Y_{i}-Z(t_{i})|=|\dfrac{1}{2!}Z''(\tau)h_{i}^{2}|,\ \tau_{i}\in[t_{i-1},t_{i}) \end{equation*} The step size in the $ith$ step has been obtained as follows \begin{equation*} h_{i}=\dfrac{1}{\alpha\sqrt[4]{N^{5}}}\left(\dfrac{2\delta_{L}}{\beta_{i-1}}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} \end{equation*} such as local error is smaller than the desired error level $\delta_{L}$ in each step of the integration.

  • $\delta_{L}$; the desired error level

  • $N$; dimension of $A=(a_{ij})\in\mathbb{R}^{NxN}$ matrix

  • $Y_{i}$; the numerical solution obtained in $ith$ step

  • $Z(t)$; the solution of $Z'(t)=AZ(t),\ Z(t_{i-1})=Y_{i-1},\ \tau_{i}\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})$

  • $\displaystyle\alpha=\max_{1\leq i,j \leq N}|a_{ij}|$

  • $\displaystyle\max_{1\leq i,j \leq N}(\sup_{\tau_{i}\in[t_{i-1},t_{i})}|z_{j}(\tau_{i})|)\leq\beta_{i-1}$

Çelik Kızılkan 2009 ,  Çelik Kızılkan and Aydın 2011
